Max Kaindl, 25, Walnut Creek

I grew up in San Mateo, California in a suburban household where a love for art and the push for excellence were always present.

I began my higher education at the University of California Irvine studying philosophy. I then transferred to the University of San Francisco to continue my philosophy studies for another year until one day I realized the futility of my studies and switched my major to fine arts. It was not until my senior year though, did I fully realize my lifeâ€™s passion: pottery.

On the wheel I try to achieve timelessness. Far after my death, the death of civilization, a piece of ceramic will still last. Archeologists find broken pottery, by nameless potters, throughout the world, linking it to civilizations thousands of years ago. The timelessness and existence beyond human experience is why I study pottery, while the mystery of the nameless potter keeps me constantly intrigued.

Studying under Arturo Araujo has taught me to appreciate the beautiful simplicity in classic Japanese forms and glazes; such as the celadons or the complex subtle beauty of shino. A fascination for wood fires and the tradition of American folk pottery though is what has begun to fuel my true fervor for pottery. I look forward to graduation with great confidence knowing I now have a sure direction in life.

I hope to pursue a masters degree in the near future.
